4.1.1 Commission / Fee Request (Commreq):
Commission / Fee Request atau biasa disebut juga dengan commission request atau commreq adalah sebuah modul di Intranet yang digunakan oleh marketing di Colliers Indonesia untuk membuat invoice dan menghitung komisi perusahaan dan karyawan.

4.1.1.1 Cara membuat "Commission Request":
Masuk ke Colliers Intranet, lalu klik "Commission/Fee Request" di menu kiri, lalu pilih jenis transaksinya apakah sewa atau jual, lalu isi form dan simpan. Email persetujuan akan dikirim ke karyawan yang bersangkutan seperti supervisor, kepala divisi dan managing director

4.1.1.2 Cara menyetujui atau menolak "Commission Request":
Masuk ke Colliers Intranet, lalu klik "Commission/Fee Request" di menu kiri, lalu klik "Commission/Fee Request Status" di menu atas, kemudian akan tampil pengjuan yang masih pending dan perlu untuk di setujui atau di tolak

4.1.2.1 Cara melihat commission performance:
Masuk ke Colliers Intranet, lalu klik link "Commission/Fee Performance Summary" dimenu atas, kemudian akan tampil tabel yang berisikan data kamu dan bahawan kamu (jika kamu seorang supervisor) serta ada kolom gaji, pengasilan yang telah di hasilkan selama periode tertentu dan lainnya

4.1.2.3 Cara menghitung target:
Target atau bisa disebut target minimun atau biasa disebut dengan "Minimum Required Company Revenue" dihitung menggunakan rumus berikut : 

Minimun Required Company Revenue = gaji x 13 x pengali.
gaji: gaji dalam sebulan
13: gaji setahun di tambah dengan THR
pengali: marketing seperti Account Executive angka pengalinya adalah 4 namun bisa berubah sesuai dengan jabatan. Jika period = 24 maka pengali harus di kali 2.

4.1.2.4 Cara menghitung target promosi:
Target promosi di hitung berdasarkan target minimum dari level diatasnya.
Contoh: Marketing A target minimumnya adalah 10.000.000/tahun dan Marketing B target minimumnya 20.000.000/tahun. Maka, Marketing A bisa di promosi menjadi Marketing B jika penghasilan selama 1 tahun dari Marketing A lebih besar atau sama dengan target minimum dari Marketing B

4.1.2.5 Kapan seseorang marketing berhak promosi ?:
Jika mencapai 100% dari target di kolom "Company Revenue Required for Promotion"

4.1.2.6 Cara menghitung performance (Actual Company Revenue):
Commission performance atau biasa disebut dengan "Actual Company Revenue" adalah penghasilan yang dihasilkan oleh seorang marketing selama 1 tahun atau 2 tahun (tergantung divisi) di bagi dengan target.

Jika karyawan itu bukan supervisor maka commission performance dihitunga berdasarkan total penghasilan individu di bagi dengan target individu itu sendiri, jika supervisor maka dihitung berdasarkan total penghasilan keseluruhan tim dibagi dengan total target tim.

Commission performance akan di hasilkan adalam bentuk %

4.1.2.7 Mengapa komisi saya ditahan ?:
Komisi marketing akan di tahan jika performance mereka kurang dari 75%. Artinya, jumlah revenue yang dihasilkan oleh marketing tersebut selama period tertentu dibawah 75% dari jumlah targetnya.

Contoh : 
Marketing A dengan performance 60% berhasil melakukan transaksi sebesar 100.000.000 juta.
Komisi ke marketing sebesar 15% dari 100.000.000 = 15 juta.
Karna nilai performance < 75% maka marketing tersebut hanya menerima 60% dari 15 juta, sedangkan sisanya sebesar 40% akan ditahan oleh perusahaan.

4.1.2.8 Apakah komisi yang ditahan akan di kembalikan / Withheld Resolution ?:
Ya, komisi yang di tahan pada tahun sebelumnya akan dikembalikan di jika nilai performance individu/tim lebih besar atau sama dengan 75% pada akhir tahun 31 Desember. Jika tidak, komisi akan hangus.
Perhitungan pengembalian komisi ini selalu dilakukan di akhir tahun dan dilakukan pembayaran ke karyawan dibeberapa bulan setelahnya. Peristiwa ini juga di sebut Withheld Resolution.

4.1.2.9 Seberapa sering seorang marketing bisa di promosi ?:
Seseorang bisa dipromosi paling banyak 2x dalam setahun untuk divisi selain CIMS, untuk CIMS sendiri hanya diperbolehkan 1x dalam 1 tahun.

4.1.3.1 Pengertian Contact Database:
Contact Database adalah salah satu modul yang berada di dalam Intranet yang berfungsi untuk menyimpan semua data kontak perusahaan. Kontak disini berupa data perusahaan beserta dengan kontak-kontak perorangan yang berasa di dalam perusahaan tersebut.

4.1.3.2 Fitur di Contact Database:
Contact Database mempunyai banyak sekali fitur antara lain: 
A. Informasi profil perusahaan
B. Informasi profil perorangan
C. Informasi mailing list
D. Informasi Lease Expiry Date (LED) dari perusahaan-perusahaan
E. Pengingat / Reminder otomatis dari sistem yang berkaitan dengan LED dan masih banyak lagi yg lain

4.1.3.3 Cara menambahkan perusahaan di Contact Database:
Untuk menambahkan perusahaan di Contact Database mudah sekali yaitu dengan cara masuk dulu ke Colliers Intranet, lalu klik link "Contact Database" di menu kiri, lalu klik link "Add Company" di menu atas, kemudian ketikkan nama perusahaan di kotak yang disediakan
lalu tekan tombol "Search Database, lalu sistem akan memeriksa apakah ada duplikasi atau tidak, jika tidak ada, maka tekan tombol "Please Continue To Add Company", mudah bukan ??

4.1.3.4 Cara edit perusahaan di Contact Database:
Untuk mengganti nama perusahaan di Contact Database bisa dilakukan dengan cara mencari perusahaan tersebut dengan menggunakan "Advanced Search" kemudian klik nama perusahaan, lalu klik "Edit Company" pada bagian bawah di halaman profil perusahaan, lalu revisi data yang ada di formulir kemudian tekan tombol "Update Company"

4.1.3.5 Cara menambahkan kontak di Contact Database:
Untuk menambahkan kontak di Contact Database anda perlu mengunjungi halaman profil perusahaan terkait, lalu di pada bagian "Services" klik tanda "+" di baris yang sesuai dengan divisi anda.
Setelah itu akan muncul jendela yang meminta anda untuk memasukkan nama dan title kontak, isi saja dengan nama kontak yang akan di input, lalu tekan tombol "Search Contact", lalu tekan tombol "Please Continue To Add New Contact", 
lalu akan ada form baru untuk memasukkan data kontak, jika sudah selesai tekan tombol "Add Contact"

4.1.3.6 Cara edit kontak di Contact Database:
Untuk mengedit atau mengganti kontak, pertama2 anda harus mencari perusahaannya terlebih dahulu, kemudian masuk ke halaman profil perusahaan, lalu masuk ke halam profil kontak, lalu tekan tombol "Edit Contact", lalu isikan data yang baru, lalu tekan tombol "Save Contact"

4.1.3.7 Cara mencari perusahaan dan kontak di Contact Database:
Untuk mencari perusahaan atau kontak di Contact Database anda bisa menggunakan fitur "Advanced Search" dengan menggunakan link berikut :
https://www.colliers.id/intranet/apps/newcontactdb/search.php , kemudian pada bagian filter pencarian isikan sesuai kebutuhan lalu tekan tombol "Search"

4.1.3.8 Cara copy kontak di Contact Database:
Untuk mengcopy atau mengambil kontak dari kontak orang lain anda harus mengunjungi halaman profil dari kontak yang ingin di-copy, dibagian bawah ada seksi "Copy This Card To", isikan catatan atau alasan mengapa anda ingin meng-copy kontak tersebut, lalu tekan tombol "Request Prospect"

4.1.3.9 Cara hapus kontak di Contact Database:
Untuk menghapus kontak dari Contact Database anda harus menjadi atau terdaftar dulu sebagai konsultan di kontak tersebut. Kunjungi halaman profil perusahaan dari kontak tersebut, pada bagian "Services", tekan tombol "Sapu" pada baris kontak yang ingin dihapus
Pengapusan kontak akan mengirimkan email notifikasi ke Syahbani untuk disetujui.

4.1.3.10 Cara hapus perusahaan di Contact Database:
Kunjungi halaman profile perusahaan, kemudian tekan tombol "Request Delete", lalu email notifikasi akan dikirim ke Syahbani untuk disetujui.

4.1.3.11 Cara menjadi konsultan di kontak Contact Database:
Untuk menjadi konsultan dari satu kontak anda perlu mengunjungi halaman profil dari kontak tersebut, kemudian klik "Edit Contact", lalu pada bagian "Consultant" pilih nama anda di dropdown yang disediakan, lalu tekan tombol "Update Contact"

4.1.3.12 Cara hapus nama saya sebagai konsultan di kontak Contact Database:
Untuk menghapus nama anda sebagai konsultan di Contact Database anda harus mengunjuni halaman profil kontak tersebut, kemudian tekan tombol "Edit Contact", hapus nama anda dari dropdown yang disediakan, lalu tekan tombol "Update Contact"


6.1.3.1 Penyelesaian Cash Advance:
Seluruh cash advance harus diselesaikan dalam waktu satu bulan dari tanggal cash advance diterima oleh karyawan. Jika cash advance tidak diselesaikan dalam waktu satu bulan, akan dilakukan pemotongan gaji.
Untuk menghindari penyelesaian cash advance melalui pemotongan gaji, setiap karyawan bertanggung jawab untuk menyelesaikan advance terkait sebelum tanggal 15 setiap bulan.
Advance dianggap selesai jika expense report telah disetujui oleh atasan karyawan yang bersangkutan hingga Finance (yaitu, semua dokumen pendukung telah lengkap dan sisa dari uang yang harus ditransfer ke rekening Perusahaan, jika ada, telah diterima) Setiap karyawan bertanggung jawab untuk mengawasi proses penyelesaian cash advance hingga pengeluaran/expense report terkait disetujui dan cash advance diselesaikan di Intranet.
Untuk cash advance yang telah diterima selama sebulan atau lebih pada tanggal 31 Juli 2014, penyelesaian harus dilakukan sebelum 15 Agustus 2014 atau pemotongan gaji akan dilakukan.

Sebagai contoh:
A. Tanggal advance diterima: 10 Juli 2014. Batas satu bulan: 10 Agustus 2014. Jika tidak diselesaikan sebelum tanggal 15 Agustus 2014, pemotongan gaji akan dilakukan.
2. Tanggal advance diterima: 20 Juli 2014. Batas satu bulan: 20 Agustus 2014. Jika tidak diselesaikan sebelum tanggal 15 September 2014, pemotongan gaji akan dilakukan.

6.1.3.2 Batas Cash Advance:
Mengingat prosedur yang terlibat dalam persetujuan cash advance dan penyelesaiannya, perusahaan membatasi permintaan cash advance minimum adalah Rp 500.000.
Tagihan/pengeluaran yang sah/diijinkan di bawah Rp 500.000 akan dibayarkan kembali melalui proses expense report

4.1.10.1 Colliers Quarterly Property Market Report Q4 2023 For Jakarta Office:

Landlords urged to innovate and adopt strategic measures amidst an ongoing recovery

Insights & recommendations:

The office market continues to be a tenant’s market, resulting in higher bargaining points for prospective tenants. The completion of the new building is adding vacant space to the significant amount that is currently on the market. New high-quality space is offered, especially in the newer building, and attempts are being made to attract tenants who have been in the same office for a long period. Besides getting a better-quality space, through good negotiations, tenants can get a better rental price for the next rental period.
The office market still favours tenants. With a large amount of available space and the completion of new buildings, tenants have more bargaining power than developers. As a result, landlords are being pushed to provide rental packages that include discounts and favourable lease terms.
Landlords are trying to provide prospective clients with better deals and are continually diversifying their approaches to attract more tenants. The direct allowance and rental reduction era ended a long time ago and other formulas and attractive allowances are being tried. Covering relocation costs is increasingly perceived as highly effective in addressing client concerns related to moving. This could be seen as a proactive measure to prevent direct price wars in the current market.

Demand:
On average, office demand is anticipated to grow about 2% per year during 2024-2025. Transactions within the CBD are projected to contribute about 63% of the total projected demand during this period.

Supply:
A large portion of the future supply will be contributed by the rescheduling of certain projects to 2024. Overall, the cumulative supply is projected to increase by about 2% per year during 2024-2025.

Rent:
Office demand is expected to strengthen, boosting landlords' confidence in rental conditions. However, average rent growth is expected to be modest, increasing by only about 1% per year during 2024-2025.

Vacancy:
Other than the supply pipeline, committed tenants will play a crucial role in reducing vacancy rates. The vacancy rate is expected to decrease by 0.2 basis points per year during 2024-2025.

Selling Price:
The performance of the office-for-lease market is expected to see the average asking selling price improve by 5% per year throughout 2024-2025.

1. No launches have occurred, and the forthcoming supply is from projects currently in the construction phase
Construction of several projects is underway, with various rates of progress. Luminary Tower has been officially completed, contributing to the cumulative supply, which now stands at 7.40 million sq m in the CBD. The new office building within the Thamrin Nine complex offers tenants in the CBD an additional option for higher-quality space. Nevertheless, the number of newly completed office buildings is expected to be very limited in the coming years.
Outside the CBD, Jakarta International Tower in West Jakarta contributed to a cumulative supply increase of around 3.75 million sq m in 2023, a 2% growth compared to 2022. Despite being in the final stages of construction, several other projects require additional time before completion and official operation. The completion and transition of these projects are expected to significantly increase cumulative supply outside the CBD, especially in 2024.

2. Despite successful deal closures, occupancy remains under pressure due to an influx of new supply
In Jakarta, about 2.90 million sq m of office space remained unabsorbed in 2023, with 67.6% of this being office buildings in the CBD. This situation presents an opportunity for tenants, especially those nearing the end of their leases, to engage in negotiations with landlords. The availability of newly completed buildings is likely to entice tenants to consider relocation. Moreover, with the business climate showing signs of improvement, some tenants are contemplating expansion and are seeking larger office spaces. Buildings with low committed occupancy levels are particularly attractive options for tenants in need of space, should landlords be unable to provide the required accommodations. As a result, tenants find themselves in a stronger bargaining position during transactions. Conversely, the completion of new office buildings will attract more tenants, boosting overall occupancy rates.

Despite the existence of significant vacant space, office demand was stronger in 2023 compared to 2022. This improvement can be attributed to the closure of certain deals that have been under
negotiation for some time. The extended duration in finalizing deals in 2023 has largely been due to the need to obtain approval from head offices, especially those located outside Indonesia. Additionally, tenants
are taking the time to assess the ongoing strengthening of the business climate before committing to agreements

In addition to transactions within existing buildings, relocations to different buildings were also documented throughout 2023. Relocations are typically prompted by disparities between tenant requirements and the available space in their current building. In certain instances, when space on the same floor is limited, landlords may propose alternative floors to accommodate the need for more space. However, from the tenant’s perspective, opting for new vacant space in the same building may incur additional costs. In response, some tenants are exploring the possibility of moving to another office building to circumvent these expenses. Notably, the current Green Building Certification has emerged as a motivating factor for relocations, especially among multinational companies.

Companies actively searching for space belong to diverse sectors, including electronics, logistics, shipping, freight forwarding, import and export, oil and law firms, and a common trend among these businesses is a preference for expansion into new spaces rather than remaining in their current buildings.

In the CBD, average occupancy was registered at 73.5% in 2023. Pressure from introducing a significant amount of additional office space saw the current average occupancy fall by about 1% compared to 2022. Similarly, in areas outside the CBD, the operation of new office buildings posed a challenge to occupancy rates. To address this, several building owners collaborated with co-working space operators. Consequently, the average occupancy in areas outside the CBD was recorded at 72.5% in 2023, a slight improvement compared to 2022.

Differing additional supply levels prompt distinct occupancy forecasts for 2024. In areas outside the CBD, substantial supply with a relatively low level of committed occupancy is likely to result in a fall in the average occupancy rate in 2024. Conversely, in the CBD, where no new office buildings have been completed and committed tenants are ready to operate, an increase in the average occupancy rate is anticipated.

The presidential election could potentially influence and bring about changes in the business climate, leading to a "wait and see" approach. Some deals may be concluded in early 2024, stemming from an active negotiation process initiated in 2023. It is expected that more transactions will be closed after the election or when the policy direction of the next government becomes known.

3.Moves to reduce discrepancy between asking and contracted rents
Some landlords have proactively reduced their published rental rates. While negotiation processes will likely follow, landlords are now contemplating a reasonable gap between the asking and transacted rent.
With substantial vacant spaces, including those in newly operating buildings, the bargaining power of tenants is undoubtedly stronger, allowing them to shape strategies and negotiate favourable terms for occupying office spaces in the coming years.
Intense competition is inevitable, especially in efforts to retain tenants whose contracts are nearing expiration. On the flip side, some landlords are actively persuading prospective tenants to relocate to higher-quality buildings by presenting enticing rental packages. In a bid to attract tenants, some landlords are even willing to cover initial costs, a significant consideration for tenants contemplating relocation. The provision of capital expenditure (capex) allowances in the tenant’s fitting-out process remains a crucial factor in bridging the agreement divide between landlords and tenants.
The provision of semi-furnished spaces for lease has become an attractive option for prospective tenants aiming to minimize initial capital costs. The expenditure involved in the early stages can often be a hurdle, leading headquarters of tenants to withhold approval for leases. In response, landlords are offering amortization solutions by incorporating the calculation of initial costs into monthly rent payments throughout the lease period.

4. Lifting asking prices takes a backseat as landlords prioritize sales volume
Due to relatively passive sales, profit-seeking actions in the secondary market have yet to significantly impact selling prices.
Asking selling prices in the office-for-sale market also improved with the higher asking prices for some buildings in the CBD bringing the average to IDR 56.3 million in 2023, a 2% increase compared to 2022. Meanwhile, the asking selling price outside the CBD remained relatively stable at IDR 45.9 million in 2023.
With strengthening occupancy levels and gradual improvements in rental rates, landlords and unit owners may consider increasing selling prices. However, the growth in asking selling prices is expected to be reasonable, with landlords and unit owners continuing to prioritize sales volume. As a result, the average asking selling prices, both in the CBD and outside the CBD, are expected to increase moderately at least until 2025.
